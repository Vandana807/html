SWAMI VIVEKANANDA

Swami Vivekananda(12 January 1863 â€“ 4 July 1902), born Narendranath Datta was an Indian Hindu monk, philosopher, author, religious teacher, and the chief disciple of the Indian mystic Ramakrishna. He was a key figure in the introduction of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world.He is credited with raising interfaith awareness and bringing Hinduism to the status of a major world religion in the late nineteenth century.Vivekananda is widely regarded as one of the greatest figures of modern India. He was one of the most influential philosophers and social reformers of his time, and the most successful missionary of Vedanta to the Western world. He was a major force in contemporary Hindu reform movements and in the development of nationalism in colonial India.He is considered to be a patriotic saint. His birthday is celebrated in India as National Youth Day.
